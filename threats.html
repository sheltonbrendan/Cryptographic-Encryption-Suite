Emerging Threats

Modern cryptography faces evolving threats from two fronts: Artificial Intelligence and Quantum Computing. Both are reshaping attack strategies and defense systems alike.

🤖 Artificial Intelligence

  • AI automates cyberattacks — phishing, malware generation, and social engineering
  • Large language models craft highly convincing scam messages at scale
  • Deepfake technology can impersonate voices and faces with alarming accuracy
  • Defenders also use AI: anomaly detection, threat intelligence, and automated response

AI amplifies both the attacker and the defender — the arms race accelerates.

⚛️ Quantum Computing

  • Quantum computers can solve problems classical computers cannot in feasible time
  • Shor's Algorithm threatens RSA and ECC public-key cryptography
  • Encrypted data captured today may be decrypted by quantum computers tomorrow
  • Symmetric encryption (AES) is less vulnerable but requires larger key sizes

This creates critical timeline pressure for migrating to quantum-safe systems.

🔐 Post-Quantum Cryptography

  • New algorithms designed to resist both classical and quantum attacks
  • Lattice-based cryptography is the leading candidate (CRYSTALS-Kyber, Dilithium)
  • NIST finalized its first post-quantum standards in 2024
  • Migration to PQC is now a priority for governments and enterprises worldwide

The goal: secure data that remains safe even in the quantum era.

// Summary

Cryptography is in a period of unprecedented disruption. AI lowers the barrier for sophisticated attacks while quantum computing threatens the mathematical foundations of modern encryption. Post-quantum cryptography is the industry's answer — but the transition must happen before quantum hardware catches up.
